The Challenge

A Healthcare System
Failing its People

Bangladesh's healthcare system faces critical systemic failures that technology can address — today.

😵

Healthcare Confusion

Patients don't understand their symptoms, leading to delayed care, panic, or unnecessary hospital visits based on misinformation.

🏥

Wrong Specialist Selection

Without guidance, patients visit the wrong department, wasting hours and delaying proper treatment — a common, costly mistake.

🌾

Rural Accessibility Gap

Over 60% of Bangladesh is rural. Qualified doctors are scarce outside cities, leaving millions without timely medical consultation.

🏟️

Hospital Overcrowding

Urban hospitals are overwhelmed with patients who could've been triaged at home, straining resources meant for critical cases.

📋

No Structured Patient Intake

Doctors receive patients with no prior symptom data, leading to inefficient consultations and incomplete medical histories.

📵

Misinformation Overload

People rely on Google or social media for health answers — getting unreliable, dangerous, or anxiety-inducing results instead.

Ethics & Safety

Responsible
by Design

🚫

Not a Doctor Replacement

MediSA is a decision-support tool, not a diagnostic platform. All outputs encourage patients to consult a qualified physician.

🛡️

No Medical Diagnosis Claims

We make zero claims of diagnosis. Every report is framed as informational guidance to support — not replace — clinical judgment.

⚙️

Decision-Support System

Designed to inform patients and assist doctors, not override them. The system provides structured data, not medical verdicts.

🔒

Safety-Focused Recommendations

Ambiguous or high-risk triage results automatically escalate to emergency care recommendations. We err on the side of caution.

👥

Human-in-the-Loop Roadmap

Future versions integrate doctor review of AI outputs before delivery to patients — a verified, clinician-validated pipeline.

🌐

Transparent AI Reasoning

Reports disclose which AI models contributed, what sources were retrieved, and the confidence level of every assessment.
